Preventive screenings are one of the most powerful tools in medicine—they catch problems early, when they're easiest to treat. But knowing which screenings you need, and when, can be confusing.

Here's a straightforward guide to the screenings recommended at every stage of adult life.

## In Your 20s and 30s

Even if you feel perfectly healthy, certain screenings establish important baselines:

- **Blood pressure** — At least every 2 years (annually if elevated)
- **Cholesterol** — Baseline check starting at age 20, then every 4–6 years if normal
- **Blood glucose** — Starting at age 35, or earlier if you have risk factors for diabetes
- **STI screening** — Based on risk factors and sexual activity
- **Skin checks** — Self-exams monthly; professional exam if you have risk factors
- **Women**: Cervical cancer screening (Pap smear) starting at age 21
- **Women**: Clinical breast exams as recommended by your provider

## In Your 40s

This is when many chronic conditions start to emerge, making regular checkups more important:

- **Blood pressure** — Annually
- **Cholesterol** — Every 4–6 years (more often if elevated)
- **Blood glucose** — Every 3 years starting at 35, or annually with risk factors
- **Women**: Mammogram screening — discuss timing with your provider (many start at 40)
- **Eye exam** — Baseline comprehensive exam at age 40
- **Colon cancer screening** — Starting at age 45 (colonoscopy or other approved method)

## In Your 50s

Screenings become more frequent and more important:

- **Colon cancer screening** — Continue on recommended schedule
- **Lung cancer screening** — If you have a significant smoking history
- **Women**: Continue mammograms as recommended
- **Men**: Discuss prostate cancer screening (PSA test) with your doctor
- **Bone density** — Women should discuss with their provider, especially post-menopause
- **Hepatitis C** — One-time screening if born between 1945 and 1965

## In Your 60s and Beyond

This is when your [Annual Wellness Visit](/blog/medicare-annual-wellness-visit) becomes especially valuable:

- **Medicare Annual Wellness Visit** — Free yearly visit to review your prevention plan
- **Colon cancer screening** — Continue through age 75
- **Abdominal aortic aneurysm** — One-time screening for men 65–75 who have ever smoked
- **Osteoporosis screening** — Women at 65, or earlier with risk factors
- **Vision and hearing** — Annual checks
- **Cognitive screening** — Part of your Medicare AWV
- **Vaccines** — Flu (annually), pneumonia, shingles, updated COVID boosters
