If you're living with two or more chronic conditions—such as diabetes, high blood pressure, heart disease, or arthritis—you know that managing your health doesn't stop when you leave the doctor's office. That's exactly why **Chronic Care Management (CCM)** exists.

## What Is Chronic Care Management?

CCM is a Medicare-covered program that provides ongoing, coordinated care between your regular office visits. Through CCM, your care team at HCI Medical Group stays connected with you throughout the month to:

- **Monitor your conditions** and check in on how you're feeling
- **Coordinate your care** between specialists, pharmacies, and other providers
- **Adjust your treatment plan** when something isn't working
- **Answer your questions** without waiting for your next appointment
- **Help you manage medications** and understand potential interactions

Think of it as having a dedicated health partner who keeps track of the big picture—so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Who Qualifies?

You may be eligible for CCM if you:

- Are enrolled in Medicare Part B
- Have **two or more chronic conditions** expected to last at least 12 months
- Provide consent to participate in the program

Common qualifying conditions include diabetes, hypertension, heart failure, COPD, chronic kidney disease, depression, and arthritis. If you're unsure whether you qualify, our team can help you find out.

## How It Works at HCI Medical Group

When you enroll in our [Chronic Care Management program](/senior-care/chronic-care), here's what happens:

1. **Comprehensive care plan** — We create a detailed, personalized plan that covers all of your conditions, medications, and goals.
2. **Monthly check-ins** — Our care coordination team contacts you regularly to see how you're doing, review symptoms, and address concerns.
3. **24/7 access** — You have access to care support for urgent questions, even outside regular office hours.
4. **Care coordination** — We communicate directly with your specialists to ensure everyone is on the same page.

## What Does It Cost?

CCM is covered by Medicare. There is typically a small copay (around 20% of the Medicare-approved amount), but for many patients, supplemental insurance covers this entirely. We'll explain your specific costs before you enroll.

## Real Benefits You'll Notice

Patients in our CCM program often tell us they feel:

- **More confident** managing their health at home
- **Less anxious** between doctor visits because they know help is a phone call away
- **Better organized** with medications and appointments
- **More connected** to their care team

Studies consistently show that CCM reduces emergency room visits and hospital admissions—because problems are caught and addressed early, before they become crises.
