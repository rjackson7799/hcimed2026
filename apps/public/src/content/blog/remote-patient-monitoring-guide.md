What if your doctor could check on your health every day—without you having to leave home? That's the idea behind **Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)**, and it's changing how we care for patients with chronic conditions.

## What Is Remote Patient Monitoring?

RPM uses simple, connected medical devices to send your health data directly to your care team at HCI Medical Group. Instead of waiting weeks or months between appointments to discover a problem, your doctor can see trends in real time and respond quickly.

Common RPM devices include:

- **Blood pressure monitors** — Automatic cuffs that transmit readings wirelessly
- **Blood glucose meters** — For patients managing diabetes
- **Pulse oximeters** — Measure blood oxygen levels
- **Digital scales** — Track weight changes (important for heart failure patients)
- **Heart rate monitors** — Continuous or periodic heart rhythm tracking

The devices are easy to use—most look just like the equipment you'd buy at a pharmacy, but they automatically send your readings to our system.

## How It Works

1. **Enrollment** — Your provider determines that RPM would benefit your care and prescribes the appropriate devices.
2. **Setup** — We provide your devices and walk you through how to use them. No tech expertise required.
3. **Daily readings** — You take your measurements as directed (usually once or twice a day). The device transmits the data automatically.
4. **Monitoring** — Our care team reviews your data regularly and watches for concerning trends.
5. **Response** — If something looks off, we reach out to you proactively—often before you even notice a problem.

## Who Benefits Most?

RPM is especially helpful for patients managing:

- **Hypertension** — Daily blood pressure tracking helps optimize medication dosing
- **Diabetes** — Glucose trends reveal patterns that office visits alone can miss
- **Heart failure** — Weight and blood pressure changes can signal fluid retention early
- **COPD** — Oxygen level monitoring catches exacerbations sooner
- **Post-surgical recovery** — Monitoring vital signs during the critical recovery period

## Is It Covered by Insurance?

Medicare covers RPM for qualifying patients. The program requires at least 16 days of device readings per month. There may be a small copay, but many supplemental insurance plans cover it. We'll explain your specific costs before you start.

## Real Impact

Patients in our [Remote Monitoring program](/senior-care/remote-monitoring) often tell us they feel safer and more connected to their care team. And the data backs it up—RPM has been shown to:

- Reduce emergency room visits
- Lower hospital readmission rates
- Improve medication adherence
- Give patients more confidence in managing their health
