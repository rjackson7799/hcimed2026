If you've recently been diagnosed with **type 2 diabetes**, you're not alone. More than 37 million Americans live with diabetes, and with the right approach, you can manage your condition and live a full, active life.

At HCI Medical Group, we work with patients every day to build diabetes management plans that fit their real lives—not just textbook recommendations.

## Understanding Your Numbers

Your doctor will track several key numbers to monitor your diabetes:

- **A1C** — A 3-month average of your blood sugar. Most people aim for below 7%, but your doctor may set a personalized target.
- **Fasting blood glucose** — Your blood sugar level after not eating overnight. The goal is typically 80–130 mg/dL.
- **Post-meal blood glucose** — Checked 1–2 hours after eating. The target is usually below 180 mg/dL.

Don't worry about memorizing all of this at once. Your care team at HCI will help you understand which numbers matter most for you.

## Lifestyle Changes That Work

Small, consistent changes have a bigger impact than dramatic overhauls:

- **Move more** — Even a 15-minute walk after meals helps lower blood sugar. You don't need a gym membership—regular walking counts.
- **Choose whole foods** — Focus on vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. You don't have to give up everything you enjoy, but portion awareness matters.
- **Stay hydrated** — Water is your best friend. Sugary drinks can spike blood sugar quickly.
- **Prioritize sleep** — Poor sleep makes blood sugar harder to control. Aim for 7–8 hours per night.

## Medication Management

Many people with type 2 diabetes take medication to help control blood sugar. If your doctor prescribes medication:

- Take it consistently, even when you feel fine
- Don't adjust doses on your own—talk to your doctor first
- Report any side effects so your plan can be adjusted

## When to Call Your Doctor

Contact HCI Medical Group if you experience:

- Blood sugar readings consistently above 250 mg/dL or below 70 mg/dL
- Increased thirst, frequent urination, or blurred vision
- Numbness or tingling in your hands or feet
- Wounds that are slow to heal
